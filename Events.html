Diamond Chronicles: Events

2023



"Feliz Cumpleaños Señora Pamela"

On the 21st of September, we celebrated the birthday of our one & only adviser, Ma'am Pamela Pelayo . The surprise went successful as we delivered our appreciation with love letters, decorations, gifts, and cookies. Ma'am Pamela expressed her gratitude through a memorable and inspiring message about her childhood, life struggles, and difficult battles. p.s some tears were shed.



"Meeting Billy Boy"

Our class went on a journey of watching and reviewing a movie called "Marka" alongside other grade levels on November 18. The movie promoted anti-bullying programs and addressed its problematic effects on our mental, emotional and physical health. We could say that watching a movie together as a whole class was a whole fun experience.



"Sino ka?"

Our school celebrates Language Week every month of November and on the 30th, we dressed up as our favorite characters from books and movies. We also joined an "Open Door" competition where we'll decorate our classroom door like a cover of a book with recyclable materials. The morning was filled with exclaims and squeals as we revealed the character we chose to dress up as, asking each other the common question that day, "Sino ka?" p.s we didn't win the competition hehe .



"Game na ba? Sure na ba?"

Known as one of the school year's highlights, our class became part of the red team this year or as we call it James Red. With numerous athletes in our section, some of us commonly joined in basketball, volleyball, table tennis, badminton, and laro ng lahi. Despite the fact that the day ended with body aches and muscle pain, we all went home with fun memories to look back upon.



"All I want for Christmas is..."

As another year comes to an end, we couldn't welcome a new season without properly saying goodbye to each other. In the morning of December 15, 8 - Diamond conducted our Year End Party with the theme of Favorite Music Song/Album. The day was filled with laughter, squeals, and loud voices as we played many games like Pinoy Henyo and Mafia Game. After eating, we exchanged our christmas gifts and ended the day with a cute class picture.



"Down the Rabbit Hole"

Wait, wait, wait. There's more! 2023 didn't want to end without a fun bang for us to celebrate and remember because on the night of December 15, we joined our very first High School Students' Night with the theme of Alice in Wonderland. The night was full of loud music, dancing, singing crowds, random screams, and colorful (severly blinding) lights. Despite the feet bruises, (possible) ankle sprains, hoarse throat, exhaustion, smudged make-up, and sweaty clothes, we would still all agree that this is how a school year's suppose to end.



2024


"Adventure with who?"

Another highlight of the school year, school field trip. From Cabanatuan to Bataan, our school went on an adventure, exploring different kinds of animals and places. Sharing foods in the bus, funny conversations, hilo moments, sleepy moments, and loud moments was enough to deepen our friendships and connection. We would say that this tour is a good start of a new year.



"Oppa Day!"

Happy Sir Jeric Day! To celebrate the birth of our handsome co-adviser, we surprised him with a cake, a letter, and sang him a song to express our gratitude. It was simple but we made sure to put our efforts (literally) and hearts in the gifts we gave him. Because c'mon, we can't forget to celebrate the birth of the most handsome man on Earth, right?
